# GCP Triple Crown Prep Framework

Welcome to the AI-powered, modular framework designed to help you master the "Triple Crown" of Google Cloud Platform certifications:
1. **Professional Cloud Architect (PCA)**
2. **Professional Data Engineer (PDE)**
3. **Professional Machine Learning Engineer (MLE)**

Target Completion Timeline: **12 Weeks**

---

## 🚀 Why Use This Framework?

Preparing for three professional-level GCP certifications requires more than just reading documentation—it requires synthesizing infrastructure, data, and ML concepts into cohesive architectures. 

This repository acts as your **personal AI study coach and progress tracker.** It is designed to:
- **Prevent Context Switching:** Keep all your notes, schedules, and mock exams in one unified environment.
- **Enforce the Well-Architected Framework:** Automatically structure your thinking around Google's core six pillars (Security, Cost, Reliability, etc.) for every service.
- **Blend Knowledge Acquisition with Scenario Synthesis:** Use AI skills to generate both rote-memorization flashcards and complex, multi-paragraph case study questions that mimic the real exam.
- **Track Synergies:** Highlight overlapping concepts across the exams (e.g., IAM is tested heavily in all three) to reduce redundant studying.

---

## 🛠 What It Provides

The repository is structured to separate your planning, raw content, and the AI agent skills that power your study sessions:

- **`.agents/skills/`**: The "brains" of the framework. These are specific persona definitions you can invoke with an AI assistant to process your study material.
  - `note_taker.md`: Distills your raw notes into architectural summaries and exacts fact-based "Flashcards" for spaced repetition.
  - `question_creator.md`: Generates complex, scenario-based multiple-choice questions aligned with official Google case studies.
  - `exam_trainer.md`: Acts as a mock proctor, scoring your answers and updating your metrics.
- **`content/`**: Your compiled knowledge base. Contains the master `curriculum.md` and where your distilled notes will live.
- **`planner/`**: 
  - `schedule.md`: The macro 12-week syllabus.
  - `daily/`: Micro daily task lists (e.g., `week1_day1.md`) mapping to 2-hour study blocks.
- **`tracker/`**: Contains `metrics.json` and daily logs to track your mock exam scores and clearly identify weak areas.

## 📖 How to Use This Framework

### 1. Set the Foundation
- Review the `content/curriculum.md` to understand the weekly breakdown and the cross-certification synergies.
- Look at `planner/schedule.md` to see the macro timeline.

### 2. Daily Execution
- Open the daily task file (e.g., `planner/daily/week1_day1.md`).
- Execute the reading or hands-on labs listed.
- **Invoke the Note-Taker:** Once you finish reading a topic, invoke the `Note-Taker Skill` (referencing `.agents/skills/note_taker.md`) and provide it with your raw unstructured notes or doc snippets. It will output a clean architectural summary and a separate set of flashcards. Save these in the `content/` folder.

### 3. Review & Memorization
- For the first 3 days of a weekly module, focus on knowledge acquisition.
- Drill the Flashcards generated by the Note-Taker to memorize the factual elements (Limits, Quotas, Ports, IAM roles).

### 4. Scenario Drilling
- For days 4-7 of a weekly module, pivot to synthesis.
- **Invoke the Question Creator:** Ask the AI to use the `Question Creator Skill` focusing on the week's topic (e.g., "Generate 5 scenario questions on VPC Networking"). 
- Answer the questions, and use the detailed explanations to fill knowledge gaps.

### 5. Mock Exams & Tracking
- At the end of each major phase (Weeks 4, 7, and 12), invoke the `Exam Trainer Skill`.
- Take a 50-question mock exam. Have the AI log your weak areas into `tracker/metrics.json` and adjust the next week's planner accordingly.
